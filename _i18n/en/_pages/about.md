I am a PhD researcher working with Clay Holroyd as part of the [Learning and Cognitive Control Lab](https://lccl.ugent.be/), at Ghent University, in Belgium. My research is focused on investigating the computational and neural mechanisms implementing sequential decision making (particularly foraging behavior), learning, and cognitive control. To answer those questions I use neuroimaging (fMRI, EEG) and computational modelling.

Before joining Ghent University, I completed my masters in Biomedical Engineering at University of Lübeck, in Germany, where I worked at the [Life Span group](https://www.ipsy1.uni-luebeck.de/forschung/ag-bunzeck/) led by Nico Bunzeck. And before that I got an Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering degree at [UTPL](https://www.utpl.edu.ec/), in Loja, Ecuador, where I worked with [Manuel Quiñones](https://investigacion.utpl.edu.ec/mfquinonez) on designing and implementing wireless sensor networks.
